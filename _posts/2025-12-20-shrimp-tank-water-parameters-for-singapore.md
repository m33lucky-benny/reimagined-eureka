## Why Water Parameters Matter for Shrimp Tanks

Shrimp don't tolerate sudden changes well. Unlike fish, they live directly in contact with the substrate and water, making them extremely sensitive to:

- pH swings  
- Mineral imbalance (GH & TDS)  
- Temperature stress  
- Inconsistent water changes  

Most shrimp tank failures happen not because shrimp are "hard," but because **parameters are unstable or unsuitable for the species**.

## Understanding Singapore Tap Water

Before we discuss ideal parameters, it's important to understand what you're working with in Singapore.

Singapore tap water is safe for humans, but for Caridina shrimp it often has:
- Higher and inconsistent TDS (total dissolved solids)
- Unstable mineral composition that varies daily
- pH unsuitable for acidic Caridina setups
- Chlorine and chloramines that require treatment

Even when treated with water conditioner, tap water can vary from day to day, making it difficult to maintain the stable parameters Caridina shrimp need.

This is why most successful Singapore shrimp keepers use RO water instead.

## Recommended Water Parameters for Caridina Shrimp

These parameters are **beginner-safe**, **Singapore-tested**, and suitable for long-term Caridina health.

### Ideal Caridina Shrimp Water Parameters

- **pH:** 5.5 - 6.5
- **GH (General Hardness):** 4 â€“ 6  
- **KH (Carbonate Hardness):** 0 â€“ 1  
- **TDS (Total Dissolved Solids):** 100 â€“ 120 ppm  
- **Temperature:** 22 â€“ 25Â°C (ideal range)

### What These Numbers Mean

**pH (5.5 - 6-5):** This acidic range mimics the natural soft water habitats where Caridina shrimp originate. Outside this range, molting problems and breeding issues occur.

**GH (4-6):** General Hardness measures calcium and magnesium. Shrimp need these minerals for shell development and molting. Too low causes molting failure; too high stresses the shrimp.

**KH (0-1):** Carbonate Hardness should be near zero for Caridina. High KH prevents the water from maintaining the acidic pH these shrimp need.

**TDS (100-120 ppm):** Total Dissolved Solids measures everything in the water. This range provides necessary minerals without overloading the shrimp's system.

**Temperature (23-25Â°C):** This cool range maintains high oxygen levels and prevents bacterial blooms common in warmer water.

Keeping parameters within this range promotes:
- Proper molting  
- Strong survival rates  
- Stable breeding conditions

## Before Adding Shrimp: The Cycling Process

**This is the most important step beginners skipâ€”and it's why shrimp die in the first week.**

Tank cycling establishes beneficial bacteria that process ammonia and nitrites (both toxic to shrimp) into safer nitrates.

### How Long Does Cycling Take?
- **Minimum: 4-6 weeks**
- **Recommended: 6-8 weeks for Caridina**

Caridina shrimp are more sensitive than Neocaridina, so rushing the cycling process is a guaranteed way to lose your colony.

### Simple Cycling Process for Singapore Beginners

**Week 1-2: Setup**
1. Add substrate (ADA Aqua Soil or Geilee Active Soil)
2. Fill tank with prepared RO water (remineralized to target parameters)
3. Start bio filter
4. Add bacterial starter (optional but speeds process)
5. Add small amount of fish food to create ammonia

**Week 3-4: Monitoring**
- Test ammonia and nitrite every 3-4 days, use api test kits
- Look for ammonia spike, then gradual drop
- Nitrite spike follows ammonia drop
- Keep adding small amounts of ammonia source

**Week 5-6: Stabilization**
- Ammonia: 0 ppm
- Nitrite: 0 ppm
- Nitrate: Present (10-20 ppm is fine)
- pH stable at target range (5.3-5.5)

**Week 6+: Ready for Shrimp**
- All parameters stable for at least 1 full week
- Temperature controlled at 23-25Â°C
- No fluctuations in readings
- Water is clear (slight brown tint from soil is normal)

### What Happens If You Skip Cycling?

Without established bacteria:
- Ammonia builds up immediately from any waste
- Shrimp get stressed within hours, stop eating
- Deaths begin within 2-7 days
- "My shrimp died for no reason" syndrome (it was the cycling)

**Important:** Even if you add cycled filter media from another tank, wait at least 2-3 weeks to ensure stability in YOUR specific setup with YOUR water parameters.

Patience during cycling saves money and heartbreak later.

## The Weekly 10% Water Change (Detailed Process)

**Frequency:** Once per week  
**Amount:** 10% of tank volume

For a 30L tank, this means changing 3L of water weekly.

### Why Only 10%?

Small, consistent changes are better than large, infrequent ones because:
- Minimal parameter shift
- Less stress on shrimp
- Maintains stable bacterial colonies
- Easier to temperature match perfectly
- Reduces risk of shocking the system

### Step-by-Step Water Change Process

**24 Hours Before:**
1. Prepare 3L of RO water in a clean container
2. Add remineralizer to target TDS (110 ppm)
3. Let sit overnight to stabilize and reach room temperature

**Day of Water Change:**

1. **Test prepared water:**
   - TDS: 100-120 ppm (use TDS meter)
   - pH: 5.3-5.5 (use pH test kit)
   - Temperature: 23-25Â°C (match tank exactly)

2. **Remove 3L from tank:**
   - Use siphon or small pump
   - Avoid disturbing substrate heavily
   - Good time to remove any dead plant matter or debris
   - Save removed water to check parameters if needed

3. **Add new water slowly:**
   - Pour over 20-30 minutes minimum
   - Use airline tubing drip method, or
   - Pour gently against tank glass to reduce disturbance
   - Never dump water directly onto shrimp

4. **Observe shrimp for 1 hour after:**
   - Should resume normal foraging activity
   - If hiding or swimming frantically, water parameters might be off
   - If behavior is normal, water change was successful

### What If Parameters Don't Match?

**If new water TDS is off:**
- Don't add it to the tank yet
- Adjust remineralizer amount up or down
- Retest before proceeding

**If temperature is off by more than 1Â°C:**
- Wait for it to match tank temperature
- Even 2Â°C difference can stress shrimp significantly
- Use aquarium heater to warm new water if needed

**Better to delay the water change by a day than add mismatched water.**

### When to Skip Water Changes

Consider skipping or delaying if:
- During peak molting period (many shrimp molting at once)
- Within 48 hours of adding new shrimp to tank
- Parameters are perfectly stable and tank is very lightly stocked
- Shrimp are actively berried (carrying eggs) and sensitive

**Note:** Mature, well-balanced tanks (6+ months old) with low bioload and heavy plant growth sometimes need less frequent water changes. Monitor parameters weekly to understand your specific tank's needs.
